Knife

Design

The construction and the technology of the knife was the most significant contribution to the project. I researched and asked the tutors how to implement each interaction before I dived into coding. First, I worked on the technology side by implementing the capacitive touch, vibration, sound, and then the Neopixels separately. After understanding the different technologies, I combined it together and made sure that it was working.

Following the coding part of the knife, I used the initial prototype (Cardboard Knife) created by Claire and Jai as a guideline to create the form of the knife. I decided to create the whole knife out of moldable play-doh and first created 3 different cardboard cutouts varying in sizes. The blade was created using two rolled out sheets of moldable play-doh that was stuck together to create a hollow center for any wires that would be needed to thread through. The handle was created by dying the play-doh gray while it was still soft and malleable then wrapped around a rolling pin to create a cylinder shape whilst it was drying.

Capacitive Touch

Dough

As I already implemented and understood the capacitive touch sensor for the knife, I applied it on the pizza dough in a larger scale. Instead of having one point of contact to sense the touch, the dough needed eight different sensors one for every slice so that the sauce can be projected onto each slice.

Reflection on my contributions

Overall, I feel I had consistently contributed towards the project throughout the whole semester. I was able to learn Arduino which I definitely wanted to learn at the beginning of the semester and enjoy working with a team that I have never worked with. To be able to see the outcome of the work that I put in, I feel very happy with the whole project. However, there were at times where the workload was uneven between the members especially for the prototype where Ali and I did most of the work.

In terms of the contribution for my work, looking back there are some improvements that I would make reduce my delivery time for the knife. During the construction of the handle for the knife, I took too much time redesigning and remaking it because sometimes it was not wide enough to fit the wires and then it was not long enough to fit everything inside so I always had to melt the poly-doh down and construct it again. Next time, I would definitely measure out everything before and then I'll start the construction. Another issue that I had trouble with was soldering the connection and wires together, I was very inexperienced and novice when it came to soldering and it took many attempts to get it correct but now I do have more confidence in understanding how to solder and knowing the importance of the errors that can be made.
